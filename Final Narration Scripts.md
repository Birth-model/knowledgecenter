# Birth Model Knowledge Center — Narration Scripts

These scripts are designed for ElevenLabs text-to-speech. Each script is timed to match the animation duration. Speak at a moderate, professional pace (~150 words per minute).

---

## GIF 1: Epic Integration

From any patient chart in Epic Stork, click More Activities and Birth Model. View Patient opens this specific patient's details right in Birth Model. Or choose Motherboard to see all your patients in a new window. You can also access the Motherboard directly from Epic's Greaseboard — just click the Birth Model button. You can keep charting in Epic while Birth Model runs alongside.

---

## GIF 2: Patient Arrives

For any patient just enter their details in Epic as usual. Once you complete steps in Epic, the patient automatically appears in Birth Model. No extra steps required.

---

## GIF 3: C-Section Arrives

C-sections work the same way. Just add the case in Epic. Once you confirm the case, the patient is added to Birth Model automatically — ready as soon as the patient arrives.

---

## GIF 4: Labor Unfolds

The Motherboard tracks every intervention and delivery time automatically. PPH risk scores and vitals update in real-time. Abnormal labs and blood loss get flagged instantly. Recovery tracking ensures safe, timely transfers.

---

## GIF 5: Team Mobilizes

Birth Model gives you the full picture to coordinate your team with delivery countdowns, flagged labs, risks. You'll know exactly when to mobilize the delivery team. And if necessary, call anesthesia, NICU, and blood bank.

---

## GIF 6: Nursing Handoff

Click Nursing Handoff from any patient and let Birth Model do the heavy lifting. It pulls everything from Epic automatically — obstetric history, allergies, labs, screening results, IVs and anesthesia. Oxytocin shows as a visual chart with every dose, along with the complete preeclampsia, GBS and Chorioamnionitis antibiotics treatment history. Cervical ripening agents and cervical exams all appear in order. At the top, you just need to confirm consents and add any huddle issues. Click Handoff Preview and you've got a polished document, ready for shift change or your next huddle.
---

## GIF 7: Resident Handoff (SBAR)

For residents and hospitalists, Birth Model generates a structured SBAR handoff automatically. Just click the handoff button on the motherboard or within the patient's chart. Each section pulls from Epic including the clinical picture, history, vitals, labs with flags, and action items. Don't Miss Callouts ensure nothing critical gets overlooked. Every field is editable, so tailor it to your handoff style!

---

## GIF 8: Mobile Notification Settings

The Birth Model mobile app lets you customize exactly which notifications you receive. Enable C-Section reminders, disable Incomplete Record alerts. Use Mute to silence notifications when you're not on call. When you unmute, all your settings restore automatically. Tap the bell to view your notification history with timestamps. Unread notifications are marked with a red dot. Stay informed and reduce alert fatigue.

---

## GIF 9: Provider Notifications

Notifications are grouped by type — admissions, epidurals, deliveries. As a result, high-risk alerts stand out. For privacy, no patient details show on your lock screen.

---

## GIF 10: Post-Delivery Workflow

After a delivery, Birth Model receives the data from Epic's Delivery Summary automatically. A notification prompts you to finish your documentation.

---

## GIF 11: Vaginal Delivery Note

Completing a vaginal delivery note takes seconds. Click Complete Delivery Note and Birth Model auto-fills from Epic — patient details, times, lacerations, delivery-related events, are filled as highlighted in blue. AI Diagnotes™ polishes your documentation and flags anything that's missing. Click Use This Response, then Save. Your note syncs to Epic as a signed document. Need to add something? Make changes and add an addendum directly to Epic without leaving Birth Model.

---

## GIF 12: C-Section Operative Note

C-section op notes are just as quick! Once you click Complete Op Note, all of the information from Epic including incision types, findings and delivery-related events, is auto-filled as highlighted in blue. AI Diagnotes™ polishes the note and flags missing info, even making sure you charted that an assistant was present. Save, and your signed note syncs directly to Epic. The note will have your billing codes attached. Need to add something? Send an addendum directly from Birth Model.

---

## GIF 13: Mobile Delivery Note

Finish delivery notes right from your phone. Open the Birth Model app and tap any incomplete delivery. Choose your template and auto-fill pulls patient data from Epic instantly — highlighted in blue. AI Diagnotes™ polishes your note and flags missing info. Tap Use This Response, and diagnosis codes attach automatically. Save, and it syncs to Epic. No computer needed! Finish your notes on the go or before you leave the hospital!

---

## GIF 14: Unit Intelligence

The OB-servability Dashboard automates tracking of quality metrics such as NTSV rates, hemorrhage, and lag times like decision-to-incision and registration to induction. It also tracks recovery times and neonatal outcomes. Filter by time period and demographics to identify disparities. Cases are flagged for review automatically. Clean data, better care.

**Recommended Settings:**
- Voice: Professional, warm, clear (consider "Rachel" or "Josh" voices)
- Stability: 0.50-0.65
- Clarity + Similarity Enhancement: 0.75
- Style: 0 (neutral)
- Speed: Normal to slightly slower for medical terminology

**Time/Number Reading Guide:**
- "7:00 AM" → "seven AM"
- "12:45 PM" → "twelve forty-five PM"
- "6:45 PM" → "six forty-five PM"
- "7/10" → "seven out of ten"
- "45 min" → "forty-five minutes"
- "520 mL" → "five-twenty milliliters" or "five hundred twenty milliliters"
- "8.7/26" → "eight-point-seven over twenty-six"
- "39w3d" → "thirty-nine weeks three days"
- "4cm/80%/-1" → "four centimeters, eighty percent, minus one"
- "2 mU" → "two milliunit" or "two mU"
- "20 mU" → "twenty milliunit" or "twenty mU"

**Pause Indicators:**
- Em dash (—) = brief pause (0.3-0.5 seconds)
- Period at end of paragraph = longer pause before next section (0.8-1.0 seconds)
- Comma = natural breath pause (0.2 seconds)

**Medical Acronym Glossary:**
- **AWHONN** = Association of Women's Health, Obstetric and Neonatal Nurses
- **PPH** = Postpartum Hemorrhage
- **SBAR** = Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation
- **QBL** = Quantitative Blood Loss
- **H&H** = Hemoglobin and Hematocrit
- **AROM** = Artificial Rupture of Membranes
- **GBS** = Group B Streptococcus
- **NICU** = Neonatal Intensive Care Unit
- **NTSV** = Nulliparous, Term, Singleton, Vertex
- **SVD** = Spontaneous Vaginal Delivery
- **EBL** = Estimated Blood Loss
